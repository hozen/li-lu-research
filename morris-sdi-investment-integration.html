1. Morris SDI Framework: Civilization Measurement Tool

Ian Morris "Why the West Rules--For Now" (2010) measures 14,000 years of human civilization through the Social Development Index (SDI): weighted combination of energy capture per capita, urbanization, and information throughput. SDI measures "what a society can do," not wealth distribution.

SDI 1.0 Malthusian Agricultural

10000 BCE-1750 CE. ~11,500-year flat period

  • Human + animal muscle as primary energy
  • Per capita SDI ~0.3 (near-zero growth)
  • Population balanced by disease/famine/war
  • Hard Ceiling: land carrying capacity

SDI 2.0 Industrial Fossil Fuel

1750-2000 CE. Exponential growth era

  • Coal to oil to natural gas, fossil fuel driven
  • Per capita SDI 0.3 to 1.0 (Industrial Revolution)
  • West broke through agricultural Hard Ceiling first
  • Hard Ceiling: fossil fuel finitude

SDI 3.0 Nuclear + Green Energy

2000 CE-present. Steady-state growth era

  • Nuclear + renewable energy expansion
  • SDI rising but growth rate moderating
  • AI = new energy proxy variable for 3.0 era
  • Hard Ceiling: compute + energy dual constraints
Core Insight: Morris SDI measures "energy capture," not "wealth distribution." A civilization can have very high SDI while per capita income remains low -- this is the precise meaning of the Energy Paradox, and the key to understanding China 2.5.
